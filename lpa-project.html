Label Propagation Algorithms on Balanced Stochastic Block Models

September 2023 Northwestern University R&B Feldmann Fellowship

[Note that the poster on this page is quite old - will update soon!]

This was my first real research project and NU, and I will be eternally grateful to my advisor, Prof. Miki Racz, and my PhD mentor, Shuwen Chai, for their mentorship, enthusiasm, and seemingly endless patience for my questions.

Abstract

We study the label propagation algorithm for recovering communities in a graph. In this algorithm, each of the n nodes starts with a distinct label from 1 through n; then, iteratively, each node takes on the majority label among its neighbors, with ties broken in favor of smaller labels.

This algorithm has several nice properties: it is simple, fast, model-free, and parallelizable. Yet, very little is known rigorously about when it is accurate or not.

Here, we give a near-optimal characterization of when two rounds of label propagation exactly recovers the communities in a K-community balanced stochastic block model. We complement this with extensive simulations which suggest further conjectures on the behavior of few rounds of label propagation on the stochastic block model.
