June - August 2024 Los Angeles, CA

RIPS (Research in Industrial Projects for Students) was an incredible summer experience that combined rigorous mathematical research with real-world applications in drug discovery. Beyond the technical work, it was a summer filled with amazing people, coffee-fueled discussions, and plenty of bad puns.

The RIPS Community

Being part of the 36-student cohort (selected from 4000+ applicants) was both humbling and inspiring. The program brought together students from diverse backgrounds who shared a passion for mathematics and its applications. From late-night problem-solving sessions to weekend hikes in the Santa Monica mountains, the friendships formed during RIPS continue to be some of the most meaningful from my academic journey.

Mentorship & Learning

I was incredibly fortunate to work with exceptional mentors: Fang Sun (UCLA CS PhD), Luca Ponzoni and Pat Walters from Relay Therapeutics, Susana Serna (RIPS Director), and Dima Shlyaktenko (IPAM Director). Their guidance extended far beyond the technical aspects of our project, teaching us about the intersection of academic research and industrial applications.

Beyond the Research

While the technical work was challenging and rewarding, some of my fondest memories are of the lighter moments: impromptu math discussions over coffee, team dinners exploring LA's diverse food scene, and the collective groans when someone inevitably made another terrible math pun. The balance of serious research and genuine camaraderie made RIPS a truly special experience.
