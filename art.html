Lines and Stuff

I'm a big fan of pretty colors and cool-looking math. Sadly, my artistic talent plateued around the time we made hand turkeys in the 2nd grade (and for some inexplicable reason, there seems to be vanishingly low demand for hand turkeys by random college students), so nowadays I stick to drawing the only things I can: straight lines.


I like lines partially because they're easy for the artistically unendowed (read: me) to draw, but also because they show how simple pieces can collectively form creative and compositionally rich images.


In that sense they're kind of like the ReLUs of visual art - individually they are just a linear pile of sticks, but together they can illuminate abstract spaces that elude explicit parameterization, be it human language or geometric imagination.
