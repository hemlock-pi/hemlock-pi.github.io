Hamming Distance Sequences (OEIS)

A mathematical sidequest with my friend Mithra Karamchedu that led to contributing two sequences to the Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences.

Sometimes the best mathematical discoveries happen when you're just playing around with ideas. My friend Mithra Karamchedu and I found ourselves exploring hamming distances one day, and what started as a casual investigation turned into a contribution to one of the most comprehensive mathematical databases in the world.

The hamming distance between two strings is the number of positions at which the corresponding symbols are different. It's a fundamental concept in information theory, coding theory, and many other areas of computer science and mathematics.

During our exploration, we discovered some interesting patterns and sequences that hadn't been documented before. After verifying our findings and following the proper submission process, we were able to contribute two new sequences to the Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences (OEIS).

This project represents the kind of mathematical curiosity that drives research - sometimes the most interesting discoveries come from asking "what if?" and following where that question leads, even if it's just for fun.
